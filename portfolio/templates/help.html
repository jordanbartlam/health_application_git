Help Page

Need some help with using the site? Click on one of the sections listed below for information. If you still can't find an answer send an email to 'jordan.bartlam@yahoo.com'.

- Getting Started -

Create an Account

To set up an account first navigate to the login page. Once here, click the 'Create Account' button and input the required information.

Add an Activity

To add an activity, click on the green plus icon on the navbar. From here click on 'Add Activity' from the dropdown list. This will allow you to enter information about a new activity into a form. If this activity has helped avoid paying money for alterantive forms of transport then please enter an estimate of the amount you would expect to have paid. If the activity eg. Gym has no impact on your travel costs, then leave the 'Amount Saved' field at Â£0.00.

Make a Payment

Once you have created an activity which has helped you to save money, you can then recognise this saving by making a payment into your dedicated account. You simply set up a savings account with your bank. This is your account at your bank to help you identify the savings from your activities. At a later stage and subject to appropriate regulatory approvals we will present analytics to identify potential investments in health and ESG businesses.

Make a Withdrawal

At some point you may need to withdraw cash either from the cash account. To do this click on the green plus icon on the navigation bar and select 'Make Withdrawal'. From here you can then enter the date of withdrawal and the amount you wish to withdraw. You will clearly then be withdrawing money from your own dedicated account but by identifying withdrawals on the site it will help you maintain an accurate record of your balances.

Update Profile

If you need to update your profile information eg. username, first name etc then click on the user button next to the Life Points logo in the far left of the navbar. You can then press on 'Update Profile' to make changes to the user information.



- Using the Dashboards -

Health Dashboard

The health dashboard translates information you provide about your activities into useful metrics related to your health:

Longevity represents life expectancy. The average life expectancy is estimated at around 80. However, with frequent activity we can expect the life expectancy to rise. This new life expectancy is listed under 'Earned'.

Mobility is measured using a scale from 1 to 12. A mobility score of 12 is the highest attainable, and reflects exceptional fitness. Conversely, a score of 1 conveys a low level of activity.

Wellbeing is a combination of the two other measures. This helps to estimate the overall happiness. It ranges from a score of 0% to 100%.

Environmental Dashboard

The environmental dashboard illustrates how the user's activities have helped reduce carbon dioxide emissions. The dashboard shows the carbon saving from avoiding each mode of transport as well as the total from all activities. Furthermore, the total carbon emissions avoided by all users of the site is presented at the bottom of the page.

For each 12kg of carbon emission savings, users will be awarded 1 More Life Token (MLT) and 0.2MLT will be allocated to the More Life Pool. MLT tokens represent an offset of carbon output and could enable companies and employers to offset the carbon footprint of their business, and individuals to offset their carbon footprint. The More Life Pool may be used to fund expenses of the business, generate returns and provide member bonuses as well as support environmental and health initiatives.

Users may buy, sell or retire MLT by inserting the relevant amount they wish to buy, sell or retire on the Environmental Dashboard in which case transfer or retirement will occur at the then current More Life Pool price. Bought MLT will be awarded to the token holding and sold or retired MLT will be deducted.

Wealth Dashboard

The wealth dashboard shows the net amounts (payments - withdrawals) the user has put into each portfolio. The dashbaord also presents the current value for each of the portfolios. Given the low interest rate environment the cash portfolio will have a constant value. In time it will be possible to highlight potential health and ESG portfolios for added investment potential and follow these through the site.

The dashboard also provides a graphical representation of the net payments into each of the portfolios. This allows the user to monitor the timing and size of their payments or withdrawals. The user can also click on the series labels in the legend to toggle that series on or off.


- Using the Analysis Page -

Data Table

The data table provides cursory information regarding the most up-to-date performance of the portfolio and the individual investments. It shows both the current price and percentage change in price over the last day.

Portfolio Value Chart

To track how an investment in either the ESG or health tech portfolio has performed over time, we display how the value of the investment has changed over time.

Returns vs Benchmark Line Chart

We also illustrate how the portfolio or investment has performed relative to a benchmark. The benchmark we use is the S&P500, which is formed from a market-weighted average of the 500 largest publicly traded companies in the US. To make this comparison, we look at the percentage returns of the portfolio rather than on the absolute price or value.

We can also isolate just the portfolio return series by clicking on the S&P500 button in the legend.
